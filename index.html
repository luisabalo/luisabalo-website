About

PhD Candidate (Leiden University / ESA) and Scientist–Engineer (cosine measurement systems) with experience across synchrotron facilities (BESSY II/PTB, ALBA), scientific pipelines at production scale, and mission-oriented optical design studies enabling new X-ray polarimetry capabilities.

Interests: X-ray optics (SPO), compact objects (HMXBs, ULXs), timing & spectral analysis, and robust scientific software engineering.

Research

Three pillars: optics design, compact objects, and software.

Optical Design & Polarimetry

System-level optical concepts and trade studies; alternative geometries to extend polarimetric reach to new energy ranges.

Observational X-ray Astronomy

Timing and spectral studies of accreting compact objects (HMXBs, ULXs) using current missions and multi-observatory datasets.

Scientific Software

Robust, reusable research tools (pipelines, libraries, GUI). Emphasis on clean architecture, testing, and maintainability.

BLiSS

Python library for blind spectral line searches in X-ray data. Professionalised implementation: object-oriented architecture, testing, and GUI development.
